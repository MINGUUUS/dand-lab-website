Welcome

Materials discovery, accelerated by data and AI.

DAN-D Lab revolutionizes materials discovery by integrating computation, data, and AI. We focus on AI-driven materials intelligence, next-generation energy and environmental materials, and multifunctional composites.

The lab aims to be autonomous, rigorous, and warm: a place where students can learn modern computational tools, communicate openly, and grow into independent researchers.

Recruiting

Graduate and undergraduate researchers are welcome.

We are recruiting motivated MS/PhD students and undergraduate interns interested in AI-driven materials discovery, machine learning, LLMs, DFT, and molecular dynamics for sensors, all-solid-state batteries, and electrocatalysts.

Coding experience is welcome, but not required. You can learn it here if you are sincere, responsible, and ready to communicate.

Research

Three connected routes toward intelligent materials design.

Our research combines atomistic simulation, machine learning, and chemical insight to move from trial-and-error discovery toward rational, data-driven materials design.

01

Machine Learning-Accelerated Materials Intelligence

Traditional trial-and-error materials discovery is too slow and resource-intensive. We develop data-driven intelligence to rapidly navigate vast chemical spaces.

Methodology

  • Develop robust Machine Learning Interatomic Potentials (MLIPs) for energy and environmental materials.
  • Build LLM-based AI agents to extract literature data and recommend promising synthesis routes.
  • Apply diffusion-based generative AI for the inverse design of novel materials.

Expected Outcomes

Autonomous discovery workflows that reduce time and cost, yielding theoretically validated materials ready for experimental realization.

02

Computational Discovery of Next-Generation Sensor, Battery, and Electrocatalyst Materials

Next-generation electronics and sustainable energy require breakthrough materials. We pursue atomistic-level design strategies to overcome current performance limits.

Methodology

  • Sensors: optimize the 4S criteria: sensitivity, selectivity, speed, and stability.
  • Batteries: develop high-conductivity solid-state electrolytes and all-in-one computational models for all-solid-state batteries.
  • Electrocatalysts: design efficient, regenerable catalysts for OER and CER.

Expected Outcomes

Ultra-precise sensors, reliable all-solid-state batteries, and highly active catalysts for sustainable energy and environmental applications.

03

Designing Multi-Dimensional and Multi-Functional Composite Materials

Single-component systems often hit a performance ceiling. We design synergistic composites that combine multiple material types and reveal new interfacial functions.

Methodology

  • Construct composite architectures such as MOF@MOF, MOF@TMD, and MOF@CNT.
  • Optimize selections from large material libraries using computational screening.
  • Use DFT, MD, and Monte Carlo simulations to uncover interfacial interaction mechanisms.

Expected Outcomes

A rational design strategy for multifunctional architectures that outperform traditional materials.

Join Us

Build your research foundation in a lab where learning is part of the system.

DAN-D Lab welcomes undergraduate interns and graduate students who want to work at the intersection of chemical engineering, computational simulation, and AI-driven materials discovery.

Who fits well?

Sincere students who take responsibility, communicate well, and keep learning through steady effort.

Do I need coding experience?

No. If you are willing to learn, the lab will help you build coding, simulation, and AI skills step by step.

What can I work on?

AI materials intelligence, DFT/MD simulations, MLIPs, LLM agents, sensors, batteries, electrocatalysts, and composites.
